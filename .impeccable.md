## Design Context

### Users
The product is aimed at movie and TV viewers exploring titles, comparing plans, and managing personal watch behavior such as watchlists and favorites. The interface is optimized for casual browsing with high visual stimulus, quick content scanning, and detail-driven discovery across desktop and mobile.

### Brand Personality
Cinematic, premium, energetic. The interface consistently pushes a "digital cinema" mood through dramatic imagery, assertive headlines, bold calls to action, and polished motion rather than quiet utility.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual system is dark-mode first and effectively dark-mode only. Core surfaces use near-black tones such as `#000000`, `#0b0b0b`, `#141414`, and translucent dark glass panels, with `#E50914` functioning as the singular dominant accent for actions, highlights, and active states. Typography pairs `Outfit` for UI/body copy with `Newsreader` for dramatic editorial display moments. Main pages rely on large TMDB imagery, heavy gradient overlays, rounded cards, subtle borders, blurred glass treatments, and animated transitions via Framer Motion and Swiper to create an immersive streaming-product feel.

### Design Principles
1. Image-led immersion: Let large-format posters and backdrops carry emotional weight, then protect readability with layered dark gradients instead of cluttering the canvas.
2. One clear accent: Reserve the red accent for primary actions, active filters, emphasis chips, and key status moments so hierarchy stays immediate and unmistakable.
3. Premium contrast: Favor deep black surfaces, muted gray supporting text, soft white borders, and rounded panels to keep the UI feeling polished rather than busy.
4. Motion with purpose: Use animation to stage content, signal active state, and reinforce cinematic mood, while keeping interactions readable and predictable.
5. Editorial hierarchy: Use bold uppercase or serif-led headings for drama, then quieter sans-serif supporting text for scanability and long-form content.
